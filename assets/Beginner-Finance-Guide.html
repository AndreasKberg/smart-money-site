Beginner Finance Guide

Download or read this short guide to get started with budgeting, saving, and investing as a Canadian student.

1. Budgeting basics

Track income and expenses. Use a simple zero-based budget or 50/30/20 rule. Save receipts and review monthly.

2. Student bank accounts

Look for no-fee chequing, good mobile banking, and overdraft protections. Compare account perks.

3. TFSA & RRSP

TFSA is generally best for flexible savings and beginner investing; RRSP can reduce taxable income if you have earned income.

4. Saving strategies

Automate savings, start small (e.g., $20/week), and prioritize an emergency fund.

5. Investing basics

Consider low-cost index ETFs or robo-advisors; learn about fees, diversification, and long-term compounding.
