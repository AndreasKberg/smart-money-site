Beginner Personal Finance for Canadian Students: Website Plan & Content Kit

This guide outlines a complete site structure and content plan. Pages include Home, About, Contact, Blog/Articles, and Resources. Each page’s layout is described with exact titles, subtitles, body text, calls to action, and image placements. The writing uses clear, casual, encouraging language and Canadian terms (chequing account, $CDN).

Site Pages Overview

Home: Main landing page with hero image, tagline, overview of the site’s mission, and CTAs (e.g. “Get Started” or “Subscribe”).

About: Explains who runs the site and its mission (“Your student money mentor”), with a photo or graphic, social links, and a short founder bio or team intro.

Contact: Simple contact form (name, email, message) and/or email address. Encouraging text to reach out with questions or suggestions.

Blog/Articles: Landing page listing articles (title, brief intro, “Read more” links). Include a search bar or category list if possible.

Resources: Curated tools & downloads for students (budget templates, calculators, recommended apps or books), with clear links/buttons.

Home Page

Layout

Top: large hero banner. Title and tagline on the left/center; cheerful image on the right. Below: intro paragraph and CTA button. Further down: feature boxes highlighting key topics (Budgeting, Saving, Investing, Bank Accounts) each with a short blurb and icon/image. Bottom: 2–3 Latest Articles with mini-previews and "Read More" buttons. Footer: social links and newsletter sign-up.

Starter Blog Articles

Below are five key blog posts with suggested titles and complete content. Use these as full posts or summarize on the Blog landing page with “Read more” links.

1. How to Set Up Your First Budget as a Teen

Subtitle: Track your income and expenses in 5 simple steps.

A budget is basically a plan for your money – it shows what comes in and what goes out. Building your first budget might sound scary, but it’s really just about writing it down and sticking to a plan. Here’s how:

  1. List all your income sources. Include part-time pay, allowance, gifts, bursaries, and scholarships.
  2. Track your spending for a month. Keep receipts or notes for everything you spend and separate Needs vs Wants.
  3. Use a simple formula (50/30/20). Aim for roughly 50% needs, 30% wants, 20% savings/goals.
  4. Write down your budget. Use a spreadsheet or notebook and list income, expenses, and savings goals.
  5. Review and adjust monthly. Check statements and tweak categories as needed.

Call to Action: Download our Free Student Budget Template on the Resources page and start tracking.

2. Best Bank Accounts for Students

Students often qualify for no-fee chequing accounts. These usually include unlimited transactions and perks. Here’s a short comparison of common student-friendly accounts (examples):

  • RBC Advantage for Students – No monthly fee for under-25s; unlimited transactions.
  • TD Student Chequing – No monthly fee and easy online sign-up.
  • CIBC Smart for Students – No monthly fee and potential bonuses.

CTA: Use our bank comparison links to apply with referral bonuses when available.

3. How to Save Money on School Supplies

Back-to-school shopping adds up. Here are practical tips:

  • Reuse and recycle – check last year’s supplies first.
  • Stick to the list – buy only what’s required.
  • Dollar store deals – great for basic supplies.
  • Shop sales – wait a few weeks for discounts.
  • Bulk buy or donate – class-wide buys can save money.

CTA: Share your saving tip and get featured.

4. TFSA vs RRSP: Which One Should You Use?

A quick, student-friendly comparison of TFSA and RRSP, outlining who can open each, how contributions and withdrawals work, and simple guidance on when each makes sense. (Use bullet points or a mini table in the site builder.)

CTA: Use online calculators in Resources to test different scenarios.

5. How to Grow $20 a Week Consistently

Even $20/week adds up. Provide scenarios for HISA, TFSA, and long-term investing, and encourage automating the transfer. Show simple math and compounding examples.

CTA: Try our Savings Calculator in Resources to see projections.

Monetization & Ads

Combine affiliates and ads/sponsorships. Affiliate ideas include bank accounts, investment apps, budgeting tools, educational courses and books. Use affiliate links and disclose them. Sponsored content should be clearly labeled.

Note: Google Sites disallows AdSense. For Google Sites focus on affiliate links and email list growth. For builders that support AdSense (WordPress, Systeme.io) place ads in non-intrusive spots.

Tips for Free Site Builders

Systeme.io: Drag-and-drop builder, supports custom HTML and integrations.

Google Sites: Easiest to use but limited. Use built-in layouts, embed Drive files for PDFs, and use Google Forms for the contact form. Google Sites does not allow AdSense.

Keep designs simple and mobile responsive. Use headings, short paragraphs, bullets, and preview on a phone.
